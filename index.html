Magnetic fields of Cool Stars

A COOL STARS 19 splinter session


Session organizers

  • Oleg Kochukhov (Uppsala University)
  • Pascal Petit (University of Toulouse)
  • Klaus Strassmeier (Leibniz Institute for Astrophysics Potsdam)

Scientific Motivation

Magnetism is an important, yet incompletely characterized and poorly understood, ingredient of stellar physics. Magnetic fields are playing a key role in stellar evolution, including accretion processes in young stars, angular momentum loss, internal mixing. The fields of cool stars govern dynamic, energetic phenomena on stellar surfaces and significantly influence the stellar environments, including planetary systems. Understanding the cyclic behavior of cool star magnetic fields is critical for assessing possible impact of the solar variability on terrestrial climate.

An analysis of the Zeeman effect in spectral lines is the only source of direct information about the strengths and topologies of stellar magnetic fields. During recent years significant progress has been made by the magnetic broadening and Zeeman-Doppler imaging (ZDI) studies of cool stars. On the one hand, more physically refined and numerically sophisticated analysis techniques have been developed. On the other hand, the number of objects studied with these methods has increased dramatically. This allowed to establish the presence of magnetic fields in essentially all classes of cool stars and reveal unexpected trends of the field characteristics with stellar parameters. Moreover, long-term monitoring of a handful of sun-like stars yielded first direct observations of magnetic cycles. At the same time, some puzzling discrepancies between results of the application of different diagnostic methods have been identified, suggesting that certain aspects of modern observations are not fully understood or even misinterpreted.

The proposed splinter session aims to provide a comprehensive overview of recent results of the direct studies of magnetic fields at the surfaces of different classes of cool stars. A special emphasis will be also given to the discussion of reliability and consistency of different magnetic indicators and to the comparison of results obtained by independent research groups.

The session will cover the following topics:

  • Magnetic broadening and ZDI: tools for direct studies of cool star magnetic fields
  • Magnetic field topologies of active cool stars: trends with stellar parameters
  • Magnetic cycles of Sun-like stars with and without exoplanets
  • Global and local magnetic fields of pre-main sequence stars
  • Global and local magnetic fields of low-mass stars and brown dwarfs
  • Towards realistic modeling of starspot magnetic fields: lessons from solar physics
  • Benchmarking ZDI: an intercomparison of independent inversion codes

Magnetic activity has been one of the central topics of the Cool Stars conference series. Significant recent progress in the studies of cool stars magnetic fields justifies a session at Cool Star 19 devoted to this topic. This session is particularly appropriate for this conference since Uppsala University astronomers have made major contributions both to developing key methodologies for stellar magnetism studies and to designing instrumentation essential for this research.
